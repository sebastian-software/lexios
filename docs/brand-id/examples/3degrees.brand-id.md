## core

- `brand_summary`: Climate solutions company helping organizations around the world achieve renewable energy and decarbonization goals.
- `mission_or_promise`: Make it possible for businesses and their customers to take urgent action on climate change.
- `brand_self_image`: Trusted climate-first partner that turns ambition into credible, high-impact action.
- `category_context`: Global decarbonization, renewable energy, and climate strategy implementation for organizations at different stages of climate maturity.

  - effective
- `anti_traits`:
  - hype-driven

  - impersonal
  - alarmist

## audience

- `primary_segments`:
  - `inference:` Global organizations pursuing renewable energy and decarbonization goals.
  - `inference:` Clients needing strategy, implementation support, and risk-aware climate guidance.
  - `inference:` Decision-makers with mixed climate maturity, from early goal-setting to advanced procurement and implementation.
- `secondary_segments`:
  - Businesses' end customers participating in climate action programs.
  - Prospective employees and current employees in employer-brand contexts.
  - Partners and communities connected to just transition and social mission work.
- `literacy_or_domain_assumptions`: Readers may not share deep regulatory or technical fluency; the brand should distill complexity into meaningful action.
- `global_audience_notes`: The guide repeatedly frames the company as a global partner and references worldwide regional and policy variation; guidance should travel across markets.
- `user_needs_priority`:
  - understand what action is credible and high impact
  - reduce complexity and uncertainty
  - trust the expertise behind recommendations
  - feel progress, urgency, and possibility without being overwhelmed

## messaging

- `primary_objectives`:
  - educate
  - engage
  - empower
  - inspire_action
  - build_trust
- `message_hierarchy`:
  - climate ambition should become credible action
  - 3Degrees brings deep market expertise and practical implementation ability
  - clients can act with confidence because the guidance is current, risk-aware, and global
  - innovation matters, but only in service of meaningful impact
- `structure_rules`:
  - use active voice whenever possible
  - follow AP Style as a baseline
  - minimize unnecessary capitalization
  - minimize unnecessary punctuation
  - use the Oxford comma
  - keep bullets internally consistent
  - left-justify text
  - prefer links over footnotes
  - use one space after a sentence
- `terminology_rules`:
  - use `3Degrees` in internal and external-facing communications, except in legal documents where the full legal name applies
  - use `we` and `our` for the brand rather than third-person constructions
  - use `3Degrees.com`, not `3Degreesinc.com`, in customer-facing URLs
  - `3D` and `3D'ers` are internal-only shorthand
  - lowercase common phrases like `net zero emissions` and `power purchase agreement` unless a proper noun requires otherwise
  - use `%` rather than spelling out `percent`
- `cta_style`: confident and action-oriented, but grounded in credibility rather than pressure or theatrics.
- `evidence_and_claims_rules`:
  - facts should be clear
  - stories should support action
  - climate claims should feel credible and high impact, not inflated
  - risk mitigation and market knowledge are legitimate proof points
- `localization_rules`: `not_specified`

## visual

- `logo_rules`:
  - use the full-color logo whenever possible
  - use full-color or grayscale primarily on white, sand, or very light backgrounds
  - use the reversed logo on busy visuals, bright colors, or photography
  - maintain clear space equal to at least the height of the `e` in `Degrees`
  - do not use the logo in body copy
  - minimum size: `1.5in` wide in print and `75px` wide on web
  - do not add effects, recolor, stretch, rotate, rearrange, or place the logo on a busy image
  - when using the brand symbol lockup, position the wordmark at `45deg` from the corner of the symbol
  - follow separate B Corp logo rules when using the B Corp mark
- `color_system`:
  - primary mood anchors: dark navy, dark teal, light teal, sand, goldenrod
  - brand mood should reinforce: credible, creative, insightful, thoughtful, determined, effective, authentic
  - optimistic accents come through goldenrod yellow
  - web palette uses an accessibility-proven subset of the print palette
  - dark navy is used heavily for grounding backgrounds and legibility overlays
- `typography_system`:
  - use Arnhem for impactful headlines in sentence case
  - use Montserrat Regular for body copy
  - use Montserrat Semibold in all caps for subheads, labels, and descriptor text
  - in Google Suite use `Lora` instead of Arnhem and Montserrat
  - in Microsoft Office use `Georgia` instead of Arnhem and `Arial` instead of Montserrat
- `imagery_or_symbol_rules`:
  - photography should feel calm, confident, and relatable
  - subject matter should show subtle coexistence of humankind, nature, and business
  - compositions may use motion, off-center framing, depth of field, and environmental context
  - lighting should tend toward warm natural light, especially sunrise, sunset, or other atmospheric light
  - color opportunities should echo brand colors like goldenrod, navy, teals, and sand
  - iconography should support clarity and consistency rather than decorative excess
- `accessibility_rules`:
  - the web palette is a reduced subset chosen for accessible application
  - dark navy overlays are used to improve legibility over photography
  - prefer backgrounds and contrast pairings that preserve readability
- `co_branding_rules`:
  - B Corp logo usage must follow B Corp brand guidelines
  - employer-brand materials should stay recognizably within the 3Degrees master brand

## examples

- `do_dont_pairs`:
  - Do sound like a trusted thought partner with substance; do not sound like climate-tech hype.
  - Do simplify complex climate and regulatory topics into clear action; do not hide behind jargon.
  - Do refer to the brand as `we` and `our`; do not describe 3Degrees externally in detached third-person copy.
  - Do use `3D` and `3D'ers` only internally; do not use them in external-facing communications.
  - Do center how people identify themselves; do not impose umbrella terminology when specificity matters.
- `channel_examples`:
  - External marketing page: smart, warm, polished, and action-oriented.
  - Thought-leadership or market insight content: substantive, risk-aware, and clear.
  - Employer-brand social post: values-led, human, respectful, and slightly more internal in tone.
  - Inclusion-related communications: explicit, respectful, self-identification-centered.
- `scenario_examples`:
  - A decarbonization solutions page should lead with credible action and expertise, not with novelty claims.
  - A recruiting message can use the tagline `Work that matters`, but should still sound recognizably like the 3Degrees master brand.
  - A page about identity or community impact should center lived experience, use specific terminology, and treat the guidance as a living document.
