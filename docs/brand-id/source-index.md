## Why This Mix Works

- `Mailchimp` contributes the richest verbal and relationship guidance.
- `Atlassian` adds explicit tone-by-context logic across in-product moments.
- `Google` contributes clean documentation patterns for address form, imperative voice, and international readability.
- `GOV.UK` contributes institutional clarity, public-service seriousness, and governance after publication.
- `IBM` contributes brand principles that tie aesthetics to decision quality.
- `Spotify` and `OpenAI` contribute partner-facing rules where branding, legal usage, and co-branding are first-class concerns.

## Observed Corpus Biases

- Publicly available guides expose verbal rules more often than internal governance roles.
- Visual systems are often well documented, but audience psychology is less explicit there.
- Tone matrices are not universal as named artifacts, but they recur implicitly whenever guides adapt language to user state, channel, or risk level.

## Immediate Implication For Brand ID

`Brand ID` should not model "brand" as only logo + colors or only tone of voice. The corpus repeatedly treats brand definition as a stack of:

1. Brand core and worldview
2. Audience and relationship model
3. Verbal system
4. Visual system
5. Governance and allowed usage
